# Ranger Diggs

![RangerRick.webp](/img/user/Images/NPCs/RangerRick.webp)

> [!info] NPC Summary
> **Role:** Park Ranger; Frog Pond station, Boston Common
> **Affiliation:** Boston Parks
> **Location:** Boston Common — Frog Pond ranger station
> **Age:** Mid-to-late 30s
> **First Appeared:** [[Sessions/Session 03 - Squiggly Bigglies\|Session 03 - Squiggly Bigglies]]

## Description

**Appearance:** Mid-30s park ranger uniform with a name tag reading **DIGGS**. Smooth palms, no military bearing, no manual-labor calluses. Frog slime on one hand at any given moment, wiped onto his pants. Lives full-time in or near the Frog Pond station, by appearance.

**Personality:** Friendly. Not a critical thinker. Eyeballed [[NPCs/Harvard/Patricia Osei\|Patricia Osei]] in a way [[Player Characters/Chandler T. Harrow\|Chandler]] noticed. Likes the frogs. Believes satellites can read your license plate from orbit. Conspiratorial. Hospitable to anyone who plays along.

**Voice / Mannerisms:** Casual Boston, talkative when prompted. Calls people *sir* and *ma'am.* Walks away mid-conversation to chuck a frog corpse off the path.

## Session 03

[[Player Characters/Chandler T. Harrow\|Chandler]] knocked on the ranger station door at the Frog Pond posing as a Globe reporter investigating "meteorological anomalies and the impending ice age." Diggs immediately knew what he was actually there about — *"there was a cop here asking me about the same thing not too long back"* — and was happy to talk once he saw a press credential.

### What Diggs Saw on November 3

- The morning began in the 40s. Frogs in the pond were sluggish.
- He heard shouting near the **Soldier's Monument** and walked toward it.
- The temperature dropped *en route* — he could see his breath.
- The fog around the monument was **unnaturally thick** — but **only around the monument.** He didn't even register it as strange until [[Player Characters/Chandler T. Harrow\|Chandler]] asked, and then he realized it was specific.
- He found **[[NPCs/Harvard/Patricia Osei\|Patricia Osei]]** standing at the monument, sketching the frost-script that had appeared on the column.
- He took a **Polaroid** of it before it melted off.
- Many of his frogs were **dead** that morning. He's been cleaning them out of the pond for over a week.

> *"It was like the temperature just dropped. It was like I could see my own breath."*
> — **Ranger Diggs**

> *"If you can frost up the monument, you should be able to freeze over the pond. It's fresh water, so it freezes over before the harbor does."*
> — **Ranger Diggs**

He coined the working term for the frost-script:

> *"Squiggly bigglies."*
> — **Ranger Diggs**

He gave [[Player Characters/Chandler T. Harrow\|Chandler]] the original full-resolution Polaroid (O'Malley only had a photocopy). He insisted that if any other journalist or detective came asking, he'd direct them to [[Player Characters/Chandler T. Harrow\|Chandler]]'s name and the Globe.

### Conspiracy Aside

> *"You know they're always watching, right? They got satellites out there. They can read the license plate off the back of your car, I'm telling you."*
> — **Ranger Diggs**

> *"Only if your license plate pointed upward, buddy."*
> — **[[Player Characters/Chandler T. Harrow\|Chandler]]**

## What He Doesn't Know

Diggs is unawakened. The cold, the fog, the frost-script, the dead frogs — to him these are weird weather and a bad week at the pond. He has no idea he was a witness to a Winter Court (or Winter-aligned) intrusion. He's *eminently* interviewable, and he'll talk forever if you let him. The party is the only set of strangers in Boston who know what he actually saw.
