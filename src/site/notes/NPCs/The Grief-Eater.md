# The Grief-Eater

![[Grief Eater.webp]]

> [!info] NPC Summary
> **Type:** Minor Fey Entity
> **Role:** Chapter 0 Antagonist
> **Status:** Encountered — fate TBD
> **First Appeared:** Chapter 0 — The Convergence

## Description

**Appearance:** A hunched, emaciated humanoid form wrapped in tattered fabric that seems made of condensed shadow. Where its face should be, there is only a hollow void that pulls light toward it. Frost and flower petals drift constantly around its form — a sign of both Courts' influence.

**Voice:** Like someone trying to speak while sobbing — broken, wet, gasping.

---

## Nature

The Grief-Eater is not malevolent by nature — it is simply **hungry**, and Boston in the wake of the World Series loss is a feast. It slipped through a crack in the veil opened by the city's collective despair.

**Behavior:**
- Feeds on grief, causing localized hauntings
- Whispers fragments of lost hopes to those nearby
- Non-aggressive until cornered
- When threatened, lashes out with waves of despair

---

## Key Line (when defeated)

> *"The harvest was denied. The courts remember. Winter judges. Summer rages. The Green One dreams of walking..."*

---

## GM Notes

> [!warning] DM Only
> This creature's words are a significant foreshadowing hook. "The Green One dreams of walking" points toward something larger stirring in Boston's magical undercurrent. The crack in the veil — opened by Boston's collective grief — is a symptom of a deeper problem that the Voices of Boston are already aware of.
