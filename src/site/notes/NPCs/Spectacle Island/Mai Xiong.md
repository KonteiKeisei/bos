# Mai Xiong

![Mai Xiong.webp](/img/user/Images/NPCs/Mai%20Xiong.webp)

> [!info] NPC Summary
> **Role:** One of the three captives freed at Blount Fisheries
> **Affiliation:** Boston Hmong community
> **Location:** Boston
> **First Appeared:** [[Sessions/Session 02 - Telephone\|Session 02 - Telephone]]

## Description

**Appearance:** Hmong woman in her 30s. Came out of the fungal sleep last of the three captives. Intact, composed, unimpressed by any of what just happened to her.

**Personality:** Does not suffer fools. Gives exactly as much as she gets — when [[Player Characters/Lee Smith\|Lee]] got belligerent with her in writing, she hit right back, in Hmong-accented English and with a middle finger to match his. She is, however, a hockey fan, and [[Player Characters/Jacques le Coq\|Jacques]] got the unfiltered version of that enthusiasm the moment she recognized him.

**Voice / Mannerisms:** Direct. Unsentimental. Cuts through bullshit in very few words. Not above a little fan-girl energy when warranted.

---

## Background

Like [[NPCs/Spectacle Island/Sapper Ripley\|Sapper Ripley]] and [[NPCs/Spectacle Island/Monroe Tanner\|Monroe Tanner]], Mai was at Fenway Park for Game 6 of the 1986 World Series and lost consciousness after the Buckner error. She has no memory of how she arrived at Blount Fisheries.

She was the **second** of the three captives pulled free from the dark fungal growth on the wall.

## Session 02

Introduced herself to [[Player Characters/Jacques le Coq\|Jacques]] almost the moment she was standing:

> *"My, my, my, my name is Mai."*
> — **Mai Xiong**

> *"Oh — so strong. You look much stronger in real life than you do on the cards in the TV."*
> — **Mai Xiong**

Recognized the concept of *ectoplasm* from the Ghostbusters movie and immediately cross-examined [[Player Characters/Leonard Holloway\|Leonard]] on it — *"Did you just say ectoplasm? Like from the Ghostbusters movie? Is that what you're talking about?"*

When she saw [[Player Characters/Pierre-Jackson Laferrière\|Pierre]] and [[Player Characters/Jacques le Coq\|Jacques]] silently coordinating with [[Player Characters/Lee Smith\|Lee]] — because [[Player Characters/Lee Smith\|Lee]]'s new telepathy was, at that point, functionally shouting into their heads — she diagnosed it immediately:

> *"You nerds. This is some Spock bullshit. Hell no."*
> — **Mai Xiong**

### Lee on the Notepad

Because [[Player Characters/Lee Smith\|Lee]] was mute from the Grief-Eater's Wail, [[Player Characters/Chandler T. Harrow\|Chandler]] handed him a reporter's notepad and pen so he could communicate. [[Player Characters/Lee Smith\|Lee]] chose to be **belligerent and openly racist** in his written and eventually-telepathic responses to Mai. Mai dressed him down without missing a beat:

> *"My name not Sandy, your name not Everett. What's your name?"*
> — **Mai Xiong**

> *"You write like a child."*
> — **Mai Xiong**

> *"You need to respect your elders."*
> — **Mai Xiong**

When [[Player Characters/Lee Smith\|Lee]] flipped her off, she flipped him off back and moved on.

Left Spectacle Island with [[NPCs/Boston Police Department/Detective O'Malley\|Detective O'Malley]] and the Coast Guard. Returned to Boston. Has [[Player Characters/Jacques le Coq\|Jacques]]'s phone number. [[Player Characters/Lee Smith\|Lee]] does not have hers.
