# Kelsey Carey

![Kelseycarey.webp](/img/user/Images/NPCs/Kelseycarey.webp)

> [!info] NPC Summary
> **Role:** Witness to the Charlestown Cold Incident; the only person to *see* the figure
> **Affiliation:** Carey family, Charlestown
> **Location:** 14 Quarter Street, Charlestown
> **First Appeared:** Referenced in [[Sessions/Session 03 - Squiggly Bigglies\|Session 03 - Squiggly Bigglies]] (via [[NPCs/Boston Police Department/Detective O'Malley\|Detective O'Malley]]'s file)

## Description

**Appearance:** Not yet described on-screen. Daughter of [[NPCs/Charlestown/Robert Carey\|Robert Carey]] and [[NPCs/Charlestown/Sandra Carey\|Sandra Carey]]. Old enough to articulate clearly; young enough that [[NPCs/Boston Police Department/Detective O'Malley\|Detective O'Malley]] called her a "sharp kid."

**Personality:** Observant. Articulate. Waited for her father to leave the room before she gave her statement — she didn't want him to hear what she'd seen. **Did not fully go under** like the rest of the family did. Kept coming back to consciousness.

**Voice / Mannerisms:** Direct. Lets the silence around the strange parts do its own work.

---

## What She Told O'Malley

> *"It didn't go all the way under like they did. It kept coming back. I saw it. It was tall, too tall for the room, standing by the TV very still. It felt like it was writing something down — like we, as in the family, weren't the point. What we were feeling was the point."*
> — **Kelsey Carey**

> *"Sharp kid. I believe her."*
> — **[[NPCs/Boston Police Department/Detective O'Malley\|Detective O'Malley]]** *(field note, attached)*

---

## Why She Matters

Kelsey is the only witness across all three Cold Incidents who:

1. **Did not lose consciousness completely** during the encounter
2. **Saw the figure** physically present in the room
3. **Articulated its purpose** — *the family was not the point, what they were feeling was the point*

That last detail confirms what the party started to suspect at the Soldier's Monument forensics scene: the entity is doing **observation and data collection on emotional response**, not predation per se. The Carey family's grief over the World Series was the *signal,* and Kelsey was the only sensor that didn't shut off.

> [!note] What the party hasn't asked her yet
> Kelsey has not been interviewed by the party directly. If they visit 14 Quarter Street, she's there. She would likely be cooperative — but on her own terms, with her father out of the room.
