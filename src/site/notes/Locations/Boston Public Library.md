# Boston Public Library

> [!info] Location Summary
> **District / Neighborhood:** Copley Square / Back Bay
> **Type:** Public Library
> **Era:** 1986
> **Status:** Mundane & Arcane

## Description

![Boston Public Library.webp|Boston Public Library|500](/img/user/Images/Locations/Boston%20Public%20Library.webp)

Established 1848 — the first large free municipal library in the United States. Renaissance Revival architecture. Inscriptions of great historical figures adorn the facade. Grand reading rooms, manuscripts, and an extensive collection. A cultural venue hosting lectures, concerts, and exhibitions.

---

## History

Founded 1848. A landmark of public education and intellectual achievement.

---

## Key Features

- Grand reading rooms with vaulted ceilings
- Bates Hall (the famous main reading room)
- Courtyard with fountain
- Rare manuscripts and special collections

## Magical Significance

The library's collection includes texts that are not publicly catalogued — works on civic magic, the history of Unsleeping Cities, and records of Boston's supernatural guardians. Access requires knowing where to look.
