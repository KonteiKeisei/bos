# The Arnold Arboretum

> [!info] Location Summary
> **District / Neighborhood:** Jamaica Plain / Roslindale
> **Type:** Botanical Park / Living Museum
> **Era:** 1986
> **Status:** Mundane & Fey-Adjacent

## Description

![[The Arnold Arboretum.webp|The Arnold Arboretum|500]]

Established 1872 as part of a collaboration with Harvard University. A 281-acre park featuring trees, shrubs, and woody vines from around the world. Part of Boston's Emerald Necklace (Olmsted's chain of parks). Functions as a living museum with comprehensive cataloguing for scientific study and conservation.

---

## History

Founded 1872. Part of the Olmsted-designed Emerald Necklace.

---

## Key Features

- 281 acres of catalogued trees and shrubs
- Seasonal blooms
- Walking tours and educational programs
- Quiet, isolated sections of old-growth trees

## Magical Significance

Some of the older trees in the Arboretum are *very* old — older than the records suggest. Druids and nature-attuned individuals feel a watchful presence in the deeper sections, especially after dusk.
