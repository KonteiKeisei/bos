# Old North Church

> [!info] Location Summary
> **District / Neighborhood:** North End
> **Type:** Historic Church
> **Era:** 1986
> **Status:** Mundane & Spiritual

## Description

![Old North Church.webp|Old North Church|500](/img/user/Images/Locations/Old%20North%20Church.webp)

Officially Christ Church in the City of Boston. Dating to 1723. Famous for the 'one if by land, two if by sea' lantern signal that preceded Paul Revere's midnight ride. A beautiful example of Colonial Georgian architecture. Still functions as an active Episcopal congregation.

---

## History

Built 1723. Pivotal role in the American Revolution. Still an active church.

---

## Key Features

- The lantern steeple (historic signal point)
- Colonial Georgian interior
- Ancient crypts beneath the church
- Active Episcopal congregation

## Magical Significance

The crypts beneath the Old North Church are older than the church itself. The awakened note that the building sits on a convergence of two ley lines — making it a place of unusual spiritual resonance.
