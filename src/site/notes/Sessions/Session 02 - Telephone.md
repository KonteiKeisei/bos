# Session 02 — Telephone

> [!info] Session Details
> **In-Game Date:** October 28, 1986 (early morning) → November 7, 1986 (lunchtime)
> **Location:** Blount Fisheries → Boston Harbor → Boston (various) → South Shore Diner → 53 Beacon Street
> **System:** Urban Arcana / D&D 5e Homebrew
> **Players:** [[Player Characters/Chandler T. Harrow\|Chandler T. Harrow]] · [[Player Characters/Pierre-Jackson Laferrière\|Pierre]] · [[Player Characters/Lee Smith\|Lee]] · [[Player Characters/Jacques le Coq\|Jacques]] · [[Player Characters/Leonard Holloway\|Leonard]] · [[Player Characters/Bohdan Thorruk\|Bo]]

The morning after the Convergence, six people who barely know each other try to get off Spectacle Island, get paid, get some sleep, and — ten days later — get back into the same room. Everything that can be miscommunicated will be. Every motorcycle in this campaign now has opinions. A Spring Court fairy takes up residence on a garbage man's kitchen counter. And a detective slaps a manila folder onto a diner table and says the word *cold* three different ways.

---

## Part One: The Warehouse at Dawn

### Bo, Spat Out

The Grief-Eater is disintegrating dust on the factory floor and [[Player Characters/Bohdan Thorruk\|Bo]] is two hit points from unconscious, leaned over on something to hack up spores and fungal mucus. Session 1's combat didn't stop when the creature died — it kept going through Bo's lungs.

[[Player Characters/Jacques le Coq\|Jacques]] walks over. His first line establishes the working dynamic of the entire party for the next ten days:

> *"Hey, my namey. You don't look too good, huh? Give me a second, okay?"*
> — **[[Player Characters/Jacques le Coq\|Jacques]]**

Lay on Hands. Bo is immediately back to full HP. Cracks his back. Clears his throat. Nods.

> *"I don't — what was that? That was some powerful stuff."*
> — **[[Player Characters/Bohdan Thorruk\|Bo]]**

> *"You sure you ain't supposed to be a doctor instead of an enforcer?"*
> — **[[Player Characters/Chandler T. Harrow\|Chandler]]**

> *"The unhypocratic oath. First, do all harm."*
> — **[[Player Characters/Chandler T. Harrow\|Chandler]]**

### The Three Sleepers

The three people pinned to the wall by dark fungal growth are freed as the remaining mycelium dies off. They cough out the same fungal gunk Bo just did and come to in sequence.

**First up** — an ordinary-looking man in Red Sox gear. He never volunteers his name. He doesn't speak to anyone in the party, doesn't answer questions, and keeps to himself until the Coast Guard arrives. He leaves with them.

> [!note] The First Captive
> The first man out is [[NPCs/Spectacle Island/Monroe Tanner\|Monroe Tanner]]. The party does not learn his name this session. They know only that one of the three was quiet and uninterested in conversation.

**Second up** is **[[NPCs/Spectacle Island/Mai Xiong\|Mai Xiong]]** — Hmong woman, in her 30s. Stands up, surveys the warehouse, and decides immediately that the weird one in this room is [[Player Characters/Jacques le Coq\|Jacques]].

> *"My, my, my, my name is Mai."*
> — **[[NPCs/Spectacle Island/Mai Xiong\|Mai Xiong]]**

> *"Oh — so strong. You look much stronger in real life than you do on the cards in the TV."*
> — **[[NPCs/Spectacle Island/Mai Xiong\|Mai Xiong]]**

She is a hockey fan. She has been on the team's trading cards. She is not shy about it. She takes [[Player Characters/Jacques le Coq\|Jacques]]'s phone number before leaving the island.

**Third up** is **[[NPCs/Spectacle Island/Sapper Ripley\|Sapper Ripley]]** — North End, Marine, globe-and-anchor tattoo on his upper right arm, clean since '76 and defensive about it. Was watching Game 6 at Fenway, blacked out after the Buckner error.

> *"Name's Sapper. Sapper Ripley. North End. Oh, I hate mushrooms. I don't even get the stuffed ones when I go to the Cateria."*
> — **[[NPCs/Spectacle Island/Sapper Ripley\|Sapper Ripley]]**

[[Player Characters/Chandler T. Harrow\|Chandler]] clocks the globe and anchor without a roll — he was Army, he knows a Marine Corps emblem on sight. Sapper, mistaking Chandler's age, asks if he served in Korea. Chandler takes the hit.

> *"Only one of my gray hairs would put me in right now. Nam."*
> — **[[Player Characters/Chandler T. Harrow\|Chandler]]**

Sapper gives Chandler the nickname he is now stuck with: **Kit Kat** (Marine shorthand for Chandler Thomas — Kit Kat with Kraken).

### Lee on the Notepad

Because [[Player Characters/Lee Smith\|Lee]] is still mute from the Grief-Eater's Wail, [[Player Characters/Chandler T. Harrow\|Chandler]] hands him a reporter's notepad and pen. [[Player Characters/Lee Smith\|Lee]] chooses to be **belligerent and openly racist** to [[NPCs/Spectacle Island/Mai Xiong\|Mai Xiong]] as soon as she tries to address him. He writes ugly things. He flips her off. [[NPCs/Spectacle Island/Mai Xiong\|Mai Xiong]] does not miss a beat — she flips him off right back.

> *"My name not Sandy, your name not Everett. What's your name?"*
> — **[[NPCs/Spectacle Island/Mai Xiong\|Mai Xiong]]**

> *"You write like a child."*
> — **[[NPCs/Spectacle Island/Mai Xiong\|Mai Xiong]]**

> *"You need to respect your elders."*
> — **[[NPCs/Spectacle Island/Mai Xiong\|Mai Xiong]]**

When she catches [[Player Characters/Pierre-Jackson Laferrière\|Pierre]] and [[Player Characters/Jacques le Coq\|Jacques]] silently flinching at something only they can hear — [[Player Characters/Lee Smith\|Lee]]'s new telepathy, which is, at this point, roughly the volume of a megaphone inside their skulls — she diagnoses it in a glance.

> *"You nerds. This is some Spock bullshit. Hell no."*
> — **[[NPCs/Spectacle Island/Mai Xiong\|Mai Xiong]]**

None of the three captives have any memory of how they got here. Last thing they remember is the Buckner error on the Jumbotron at Fenway.

### True Forms

While [[Player Characters/Bohdan Thorruk\|Bo]] and [[Player Characters/Jacques le Coq\|Jacques]] are standing close, crouched over samples of the remaining Luminous Hollow fungi, both men look up at each other and — for a single held moment — see each other as they actually are. This is not something the rest of the party sees.

[[Player Characters/Jacques le Coq\|Jacques]] sees [[Player Characters/Bohdan Thorruk\|Bo]] as **an Orc**. Skin color, tusks, all of it. [[Player Characters/Bohdan Thorruk\|Bo]] sees [[Player Characters/Jacques le Coq\|Jacques]] as what he actually is: **a Winter-aligned Goliath**. Eight and a half feet tall. Ritual tattoos running from forehead down the neck and arms. And — notably — all his teeth.

> [!note] Awakening and Glamour
> This is the session where the party learns that fully awakened individuals can see through each other's glamours, especially under high stress or among high magic. Unawakened humans literally cannot perceive the truth — their minds will not allow it. [[Player Characters/Jacques le Coq\|Jacques]] has been awakened since birth. [[Player Characters/Bohdan Thorruk\|Bo]] has been able to do this "for a few years." The other four woke up on the night of the Convergence.

### Lee's Voice, Relocated

While Jacques and Bo are having their moment of recognition, [[Player Characters/Lee Smith\|Lee]] — still mute from the Grief-Eater's Wail — discovers something new. He can push thoughts directly into other people's heads. Loudly, at first. Two people at a time.

> *"Can you hear me? This is in Pierre and Jacques' head."*
> — **[[Player Characters/Lee Smith\|Lee]]** *(telepathic)*

> *"Okay, I think I can talk like this."*
> — **[[Player Characters/Lee Smith\|Lee]]** *(telepathic)*

[[Player Characters/Pierre-Jackson Laferrière\|Pierre]] reacts to having a voice suddenly shouted inside his skull by yelping out loud. [[Player Characters/Jacques le Coq\|Jacques]]:

> *"Why are you screaming, huh?"*
> — **[[Player Characters/Jacques le Coq\|Jacques]]** *(out loud, to someone thinking at him)*

## Part Two: Guns, Spotlights, and the Second Diatribe

### The Squib Load

[[Player Characters/Pierre-Jackson Laferrière\|Pierre]] is carrying two guns: his own, which ruptured on a natural 1 in last session's combat, and [[Player Characters/Chandler T. Harrow\|Chandler]]'s borrowed .38, which ruptured on a *different* natural 1 seconds later. [[Player Characters/Leonard Holloway\|Leonard]] asks to see.

> *"Let me take a look at that. I mean, it's probably just a squib load."*
> — **[[Player Characters/Leonard Holloway\|Leonard]]**

> *"Wait. Leonard — have you ever held a gun before?"*
> — **GM**

> *"No, but he's read manuals."*
> — **[[Player Characters/Leonard Holloway\|Leonard]]**

[[Player Characters/Chandler T. Harrow\|Chandler]] starts explaining, patient and deliberate: this is the cylinder release, this is the hammer, the hammer's two-stage, don't point that end at anything. Mid-monologue, [[Player Characters/Leonard Holloway\|Leonard]] pulls the cylinder, clicks it back in, spins it, clicks it together. Perfect. Mechanical check passed. The gun is not just fixed — it feels smooth and clean, like new.

> *"I'll be damned."*
> — **[[Player Characters/Chandler T. Harrow\|Chandler]]**

> *"You're cool, man. You know guns. You're so cool, man. You're more cool than I think you were."*
> — **[[Player Characters/Pierre-Jackson Laferrière\|Pierre]]**

[[Player Characters/Pierre-Jackson Laferrière\|Pierre]] then hands [[Player Characters/Leonard Holloway\|Leonard]] his own gun, which looks like a Looney Tunes explosion that Bugs Bunny put his finger into. Literal seawater drains out of it as Leonard turns it over. Mechanical check for Pierre's gun: no. The barrel assembly and frame both need replacing. Not this session.

> *"It was the first time I've actually ever held a gun."*
> — **[[Player Characters/Leonard Holloway\|Leonard]]**

### Coast Guard on the Harbor

Before anyone can enjoy the moment, a loudspeaker cuts through the warehouse walls.

> *"This is the U.S. Coast Guard. We have been sent here by the Boston Police Department in search of a stolen boat. Come out with your hands up."*
> — **U.S. Coast Guard**

Followed immediately by a familiar voice grabbing the microphone.

> *"Give me that fucking thing. Give me that fucking thing. This is Detective O'Malley. Chandler, if you're fucking in there, I swear to God —"*
> — **[[NPCs/Boston Police Department/Detective O'Malley\|Detective O'Malley]]**

[[Player Characters/Pierre-Jackson Laferrière\|Pierre]] hides his working gun in his boot on [[Player Characters/Chandler T. Harrow\|Chandler]]'s advice. [[Player Characters/Lee Smith\|Lee]] cracks the garage door for Chandler to negotiate, gets spotlit immediately, and retreats.

### O'Malley's Assessment

[[Player Characters/Chandler T. Harrow\|Chandler]] steps out with his hands up. [[NPCs/Boston Police Department/Detective O'Malley\|Detective O'Malley]] steps off the boat. Exchanges cycle through predictable beats — *how much do you want to know, how much do you not want to know* — and then [[NPCs/Boston Police Department/Detective O'Malley\|Detective O'Malley]] gets his first look at who else is in that warehouse.

> *"There's a garbage man, a hockey legend, a boy scout, Prince. You got fucking Prince in there, Harrow? And you adopted a kid from fucking Vietnam."*
> — **[[NPCs/Boston Police Department/Detective O'Malley\|Detective O'Malley]]**

He pulls [[Player Characters/Pierre-Jackson Laferrière\|Pierre]] aside on the accent alone, and the ghost of deportation hovers.

> *"That's definitely not a Boston accent. It's not a Louisiana accent. It's not a fucking Florida accent. So, I'm guessing you're from somewhere up north, yeah? You got a passport? You got a visa?"*
> — **[[NPCs/Boston Police Department/Detective O'Malley\|Detective O'Malley]]**

> *"He's a hockey fan."*
> — **[[Player Characters/Chandler T. Harrow\|Chandler]]**

> *"He's a — he's a hockey fan. Of course he's a fucking hockey fan."*
> — **[[NPCs/Boston Police Department/Detective O'Malley\|Detective O'Malley]]**

The moment passes. [[Player Characters/Pierre-Jackson Laferrière\|Pierre]], post-crisis, under his breath:

> *"We cool, man. We cool."*
> — **[[Player Characters/Pierre-Jackson Laferrière\|Pierre]]**

> *"Imagine if they found out you were French."*
> — **[[Player Characters/Lee Smith\|Lee]]** *(telepathic)*

### The Hockey Handshake

[[NPCs/Boston Police Department/Detective O'Malley\|Detective O'Malley]] pivots, and his tone shifts by ninety degrees, and he sticks out his hand to [[Player Characters/Jacques le Coq\|Jacques]].

> *"Give me that hand. Give me that big fucking meat mug. The beating you put down on that man in the garden. You protected. You protected our boy. You know what? I don't care what happens with the rest of these fucks. You can get me front row tickets. I got you, bro."*
> — **[[NPCs/Boston Police Department/Detective O'Malley\|Detective O'Malley]]**

> [!note] The tickets aren't for him
> [[NPCs/Boston Police Department/Detective O'Malley\|Detective O'Malley]] is asking [[Player Characters/Jacques le Coq\|Jacques]] for tickets he hasn't been given yet so that he can turn around and hand them to **[[NPCs/Boston Harbor/Captain Sandy Madiera\|Captain Madiera]]** as payment for her hauling everyone off Spectacle Island on twenty minutes' notice. He is pre-spending [[Player Characters/Jacques le Coq\|Jacques]]'s generosity to clear one of his own debts. Neither Jacques nor Madiera know this yet.

### To Burn or Not to Burn

[[NPCs/Boston Police Department/Detective O'Malley\|Detective O'Malley]] takes one look into the warehouse interior, sees the full extent of the mold-looking mushroom horror, and immediately proposes arson.

> *"Fuck a crime scene. We're gonna have to burn this fucking place down. This is a black mold infection if I've ever fucking seen one."*
> — **[[NPCs/Boston Police Department/Detective O'Malley\|Detective O'Malley]]**

> *"It's not black mold."*
> — **[[Player Characters/Bohdan Thorruk\|Bo]]**

> *"Hold on, Harrow. An expert is speaking. Mr. Garbage Man, sir. You say it's not black mold. How would you know that?"*
> — **[[NPCs/Boston Police Department/Detective O'Malley\|Detective O'Malley]]**

> *"Well, for one — it's not black."*
> — **[[Player Characters/Bohdan Thorruk\|Bo]]**

A pull-aside conversation between [[Player Characters/Jacques le Coq\|Jacques]] and [[Player Characters/Bohdan Thorruk\|Bo]] establishes that they should probably, in fact, burn it — a CSI team stumbling into the residual fey growth would be dangerous for the unawakened. [[Player Characters/Bohdan Thorruk\|Bo]] agrees, and [[Player Characters/Chandler T. Harrow\|Chandler]] sells the plan to [[NPCs/Boston Police Department/Detective O'Malley\|Detective O'Malley]].

> *"Your CSI guys aren't equipped like Venkman over there."*
> — **[[Player Characters/Chandler T. Harrow\|Chandler]]** *(nodding at [[Player Characters/Leonard Holloway\|Leonard]])*

> *"I fucking hate that movie. My kid keeps watching it. Halloween's coming up. Do you know how much a Ghostbuster uniform is?"*
> — **[[NPCs/Boston Police Department/Detective O'Malley\|Detective O'Malley]]**

## Part Three: The Circle on the Concrete

As the party moves to leave, [[Player Characters/Leonard Holloway\|Leonard]] and [[Player Characters/Bohdan Thorruk\|Bo]] both look down at a particular arrangement of phosphorescent growth on the concrete floor and say the same word at the same time. The GM notes the difference in delivery:

> [!note] Same word, different weight
> One of them said it the way a person would say something that has been passed down for generations and is a storied part of their history. The other said it like an absolute complete nerd. The word was **Fae**. What they were looking at was a **Fae circle.**

The circle pulses briefly — the Luminous Hollow fungi around its edge contracting in synchronization with something the party cannot see the other side of — and then fades. The little red-dotted mushrooms retreat into the concrete. Nothing steps through. Nothing asks anyone's name. [[Player Characters/Bohdan Thorruk\|Bo]], [[Player Characters/Jacques le Coq\|Jacques]], and [[Player Characters/Leonard Holloway\|Leonard]] exchange the look of people who have just collectively decided not to poke at it.

> *"Not again. Not again."*
> — **[[Player Characters/Jacques le Coq\|Jacques]]**

### Lee's Arson Job

[[Player Characters/Lee Smith\|Lee]] volunteers to handle the fire, professionally. He takes [[Player Characters/Pierre-Jackson Laferrière\|Pierre]]'s Zippo, walks into the warehouse office — all those flammable 80s cubicles and decades-old filing — and spends fifteen minutes setting it up properly. No smoke while he's still leaving. The party walks to the far end of the island. By the time they reach the rocky shore on the other side, the entire warehouse is collapsing on itself in flame.

## Part Four: Captain Madiera and the Harbor Crossing

### The Tugboat

The boat they stole is gone. The Coast Guard boats are gone. [[NPCs/Boston Police Department/Detective O'Malley\|Detective O'Malley]] is gone. What arrives, puttering in from the harbor mist, is a *tugboat*. Larger than anything that came to the island tonight.

At the wheel: **[[NPCs/Boston Harbor/Captain Sandy Madiera\|Captain Madiera]]**. Boston Harbor pilot boat captain. Crazy troll-doll black hair hanging out from under an old Gorton fisherman's hat. High water boots. Waterproof trench. She has been doing this her entire life, and she has been doing *this particular favor* for about twenty minutes and is already done with it.

> *"Drunk fuck O'Malley owes me big."*
> — **[[NPCs/Boston Harbor/Captain Sandy Madiera\|Captain Madiera]]** *(approximately)*

### Leonard's Hypospray

[[Player Characters/Jacques le Coq\|Jacques]] points out — out loud, to [[Player Characters/Chandler T. Harrow\|Chandler]] — that Trudy isn't on the beach anymore. The motorcycle has vanished. Which makes the explanation they're about to give [[NPCs/Boston Harbor/Captain Sandy Madiera\|Captain Madiera]] about how [[Player Characters/Pierre-Jackson Laferrière\|Pierre]] "forgot his bike" a little harder to land.

[[Player Characters/Leonard Holloway\|Leonard]] casts **Guidance** on [[Player Characters/Chandler T. Harrow\|Chandler]]. In his hands: what looks like an 80s-era EpiPen. A hypospray. He stabs [[Player Characters/Chandler T. Harrow\|Chandler]] in the back with it mid-negotiation.

> *"Just helps you focus."*
> — **[[Player Characters/Leonard Holloway\|Leonard]]**

[[Player Characters/Chandler T. Harrow\|Chandler]] rolls **Deception 18** (plus d4 from Guidance).

> *"I mean, you were driving the boat, and it's night, and I know you got your spotlight around, but it had to have been there, right?"*
> — **[[Player Characters/Chandler T. Harrow\|Chandler]]**

[[NPCs/Boston Harbor/Captain Sandy Madiera\|Captain Madiera]] accepts this.

> *"We're just going to put aside for a second that I just watched that young man stab you with something, and you were perfectly okay with it. Families get into some weird shit. Yeah, that's perfectly acceptable. I guess it could have been there."*
> — **[[NPCs/Boston Harbor/Captain Sandy Madiera\|Captain Madiera]]**

### Trudy, Amphibious

Mid-conversation, [[Player Characters/Pierre-Jackson Laferrière\|Pierre]] is still on the beach, gone offline from the main group. [[NPCs/Boston Harbor/Captain Sandy Madiera\|Captain Madiera]] swings the boat around to pick him up.

The motorcycle **comes out of the water.** [[Player Characters/Pierre-Jackson Laferrière\|Pierre]] is on it. Riding.

> *"Oh, that's how he got it on the island."*
> — **[[Player Characters/Leonard Holloway\|Leonard]]**

> *"Wow. I don't know who put those mods on Trudy, but I'm happy."*
> — **[[Player Characters/Pierre-Jackson Laferrière\|Pierre]]**

[[NPCs/Boston Harbor/Captain Sandy Madiera\|Captain Madiera]] lets them off at the harbor in Boston with the hopes she never meets any of them ever again, because the front-row tickets [[Player Characters/Jacques le Coq\|Jacques]] promised are not worth it.

## Part Five: Ten Days

> [!info] Long Rest Mechanics
> The homebrew long rest requires 72 hours in a safe haven. The party has ten days. They pass the check — everyone rests successfully. No offensive spells, no combat, light activity allowed.

The next ten days are a montage of six separate stories. They interlace loosely — Lee is sleeping in the presidential suite of [[Player Characters/Jacques le Coq\|Jacques]]'s hotel for most of it; [[Player Characters/Bohdan Thorruk\|Bo]] is alone in his kitchen — but nobody sees anyone else regularly until the diner meeting on Day 8.

### Leonard — The Hospital, the Library, the Society

[[Player Characters/Leonard Holloway\|Leonard]] visits [[NPCs/Boston Paranormal Society/Diane Okoye\|Diane]] every day at Mass General. She is in **Room 372**, still comatose, and stays that way for the entirety of the ten days.

At the Paranormal Society, he talks. A lot. About Spectacle Island, about the ghost at Long Wharf, about the fungi, about the green flash, about everything he now has clearance to say out loud. The Society does not receive it well.

[[NPCs/Boston Paranormal Society/Gary Phelps\|Gary Phelps]] blames him, specifically and openly, for what happened to [[NPCs/Boston Paranormal Society/Diane Okoye\|Diane]] — for dragging her out to Long Wharf on readings he built with his own hands, and for coming back from it without her. Phelps isn't buying the ghost story. None of them are.

> [!note] Society Fallout
> **[[NPCs/Boston Paranormal Society/Gary Phelps\|Gary Phelps]] is especially angry at [[Player Characters/Leonard Holloway\|Leonard]]** for getting [[NPCs/Boston Paranormal Society/Diane Okoye\|Diane]] nearly killed. No member of the Boston Paranormal Society believes Leonard's account that a ghost attacked her. Some of them have stopped returning his calls entirely.

He spends the rest of the time in the library. Shakespeare's *A Midsummer Night's Dream*. Celtic folklore. Anything referencing the Fae. GM verdict: he gains **advantage on any Fae-related history checks until his next long rest.** Specific facts are not granted — this is research, not revelation.

### Chandler — Margaret Drummond

[[Player Characters/Chandler T. Harrow\|Chandler]] pulls on the thread he's had since before the campaign started: the deaths of the men from **Phan Lộc**. Drummond dead in Fort Point Channel with no water in his lungs. Tillman missing. Vance erased. Chandler is the last one standing.

**Investigation check: 16.** He finds Drummond's widow: **[[NPCs/Brookline/Margaret Drummond\|Margaret Drummond]]**, 58, Brookline. Through [[NPCs/Boston Police Department/Detective O'Malley\|Detective O'Malley]] he gets the police statement she gave — three sentences. The nightmares, the drinking after 15 years sober, nothing else. She never told them what the nightmares were *about.*

He drives out to Brookline. Condolences. Soft questions. She opens up carefully.

> *"Wesley never talked about Vietnam. Not once. Not to me, not to anyone. The nightmares started immediately and never fully stopped, but I knew better than to push. He was a good husband, a good father, went to Mass every Sunday."*
> — **[[NPCs/Brookline/Margaret Drummond\|Margaret Drummond]]**

Then she pauses.

> *"But three weeks before he died, he started saying a word in his sleep. Fan lock. Fan lock. I'd never heard it before. I looked it up and I found nothing on it. I don't know what it means or why my husband was saying it in his sleep for three weeks before he walked into that damned canal."*
> — **[[NPCs/Brookline/Margaret Drummond\|Margaret Drummond]]**

> [!note] Phan Lộc
> **"Fan lock"** is Margaret's phonetic rendering of **Phan Lộc** — the Vietnamese village where [[Player Characters/Chandler T. Harrow\|Chandler]] wrote the massacre into a firefight. They are pronounced the same. She has no way of knowing this. The men Chandler falsified reports for are now dreaming the name in their sleep in an English phonetic spelling of a Vietnamese place name — before dying in dry lungs full of nothing.

She mentions **Roy Tillman** stopped by back in August for an afternoon — beer on the porch, dinner, gone in the evening. Nothing unusual.

[[Player Characters/Chandler T. Harrow\|Chandler]] tries to **Columbo** her — *"Just one more thing, ma'am"* — asking to see the bedroom and Drummond's military effects. She shuts it down.

> *"Young man, I know that people think that when others age, certain needs and drives disappear — the need to be close to one another. We did not have separate rooms. I am not Lucille Ball."*
> — **[[NPCs/Brookline/Margaret Drummond\|Margaret Drummond]]**

Persuasion fails. He thanks her. He visits the Ace Hardware in South Brookline where Drummond was manager — the staff loved him. Community drives through his church. Fair prices. Integrity. No secrets there either.

### Pierre — Trudy, Trudy, Trudy

[[Player Characters/Pierre-Jackson Laferrière\|Pierre]] takes [[NPCs/Boston Harbor/Trudy\|Trudy]] out for test rides starting day six or seven. She behaves like a large predatory cat: sometimes aligned with what he wants, sometimes deciding on her own to cross six lanes of traffic, run a red light, and nearly cause an accident.

> *"You two have a lot of the same ideas. 'Let's go for a ride, let's look at this view, let's hit the place on the way home.' But sometimes she'll turn down a different street."*
> — **GM**

On Day 9, he scouts his old apartment. **The Diablos Red Saints are not watching the place.** They believe he is dead. The neighborhood is clear.

He goes to the bakery below his apartment — **[[Locations/Boval\|Boval]]** — and is recognized immediately by the patron and his daughter, **[[NPCs/Bova/Marceline\|Marceline]]**, who have heard the story of his "death" and refuse to let him pay for the croissants.

> *"I'll still drop a five to the daughter. Hey, I'll let you have my key to my place. Just make sure the dishes are done, because I don't think I did the dishes when I left last time."*
> — **[[Player Characters/Pierre-Jackson Laferrière\|Pierre]]**

> *"Who do you think I am? I am not your maid."*
> — **[[NPCs/Bova/Marceline\|Marceline]]**

A second five dollars changes her mind.

> *"Fine. I shall do these for you. Just these once. Do not make a habit of it."*
> — **[[NPCs/Bova/Marceline\|Marceline]]**

### Bo — The Spring Court Arrives

This is the big one, and it happens at the **tail end** of the ten days — effectively the morning the party is reconvening on November 7, when [[Player Characters/Bohdan Thorruk\|Bo]] finally gets around to working on the samples he's been carrying.

He's alone in his kitchen. It smells of controlled decay — kimchi, sauerkraut, kombucha — and of a cat that doesn't move much. He uncaps the two sample vials he collected at Blount Fisheries side by side. The Luminous Hollow specimen **leaps out of its container**, consumes the dark growth specimen whole, and slithers back into itself faster than a mycological organism should be able to move.

And then the embers start.

A Fae circle begins to form on his kitchen counter. It hits the edge of a cast-iron pan, **recoils**, and reorients — the whole pattern shifts three inches to the side before completing itself. A small winged woman steps out. Feral. Impatient. Does not volunteer a name.

> *"I'm cleaning up. This doesn't belong here. I'm doing you a favor by getting rid of it."*
> — **The Fey**

She looks at [[Player Characters/Bohdan Thorruk\|Bo]], alone in his kitchen, and jerks her head at the sample jar.

> *"Are you going to sit there, or are you going to help me?"*
> — **The Fey**

[[Player Characters/Bohdan Thorruk\|Bo]], being careful with his words, agrees. She instantly locks him into owing a favor in return.

> *"You agree, so I am doing you a favor. Wonderful."*
> — **The Fey**

He places the sample jar into the circle. It vanishes.

The circle does not vanish.
