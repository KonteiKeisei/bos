# Session 03 — Squiggly Bigglies

> [!info] Session Details
> **In-Game Date:** November 7–12, 1986 (afternoon → late afternoon)
> **Location:** 53 Beacon Street → Boston Common (Soldier's Monument & Frog Pond) → Walgreens → Bromfield Camera → Bo's apartment
> **System:** Urban Arcana / D&D 5e Homebrew
> **Players:** [[Player Characters/Chandler T. Harrow\|Chandler T. Harrow]] · [[Player Characters/Lee Smith\|Lee]] · [[Player Characters/Jacques le Coq\|Jacques]] · [[Player Characters/Leonard Holloway\|Leonard]] · [[Player Characters/Bohdan Thorruk\|Bo]] *(JoJo absent — [[Player Characters/Pierre-Jackson Laferrière\|Pierre]] is offscreen, walking [[NPCs/Boston Harbor/Trudy\|Trudy]] like a dog through downtown Boston)*

Five strangers turned detectives spend an afternoon and an evening turning [[NPCs/Boston Police Department/Detective O'Malley\|Detective O'Malley]]'s manila folder into an actual case file. They find a magical crime scene in plain sight on Boston Common, identify the writing as something close to Sylvan, set a dinner appointment with a Harvard public-relations officer who got hit by it personally, and end the day with [[Player Characters/Bohdan Thorruk\|Bo]] declaring open war on the Summer Court via a bottle of iron-laced kombucha. The session opens with a phone call about garbage and closes with a Fae embassy banished mid-party. The Patricia Osei interview is still pending. So is the world.

## Part Two: The File

[[Player Characters/Chandler T. Harrow\|Chandler]] opens the manila folder [[NPCs/Boston Police Department/Detective O'Malley\|Detective O'Malley]] gave him at the South Shore Diner. The party reads in silence and then puts pushpins on Chandler's map.

### Three Cold Incidents

The case file contains:

**Incident 1 — October 29, 912 Massachusetts Avenue, Cambridge.** Witness: **[[NPCs/Cambridge/Dennis Fahey\|Dennis Fahey]]**. O'Malley's interview notes. Discharged from **Cambridge City Hospital** before he could be fully questioned.

**Incident 2 — October 31, 14 Quarter Street, Charlestown.** Witnesses: **[[NPCs/Charlestown/Robert Carey\|Robert Carey]]** (44, longshoreman) and his daughter **[[NPCs/Charlestown/Kelsey Carey\|Kelsey Carey]]**. Across the street at 12 Quarter Street: **[[NPCs/Charlestown/Ruth Hanlon\|Ruth Hanlon]]** (71, uncooperative).

> *"The cold came from the corner of where the TV was. Not a draft. From the corner. It felt like something came in that doesn't need a window. The cold had a shape. Couldn't see it clear, but the way it moved, it wasn't like air. It knew where it was going."*
> — **[[NPCs/Charlestown/Robert Carey\|Robert Carey]]** *(via O'Malley's interview notes)*

> *"It was tall, too tall for the room, standing by the TV very still. It felt like it was writing something down, like we — the family — weren't the point. What we were feeling was the point."*
> — **[[NPCs/Charlestown/Kelsey Carey\|Kelsey Carey]]** *(O'Malley notes: "sharp kid, I believe her")*

> *"That shit doesn't pick a spot and stay there. I'm 71. I grew up on this street."*
> — **[[NPCs/Charlestown/Ruth Hanlon\|Ruth Hanlon]]** *(O'Malley got nothing else out of her)*

**Incident 3 — November 3, Soldier's Monument, Boston Common.** Witness: **[[NPCs/Harvard/Patricia Osei\|Patricia Osei]]** (Harvard). Was jogging past the monument when she felt the cold. Sketched the writing she saw on the column. Park ranger **[[NPCs/Boston Common/Ranger Diggs\|Diggs]]** photographed it with a Polaroid before it melted. O'Malley wrote the report after a few too many drinks — half the page is unreadable.

> *"Drunk and Irish, and I can't tell which one's worse."*
> — **[[Player Characters/Chandler T. Harrow\|Chandler]]** *(on O'Malley's penmanship)*

### Pinning the Map

[[Player Characters/Chandler T. Harrow\|Chandler]]'s wall map gets four pushpins: the three cold incidents, plus [[NPCs/Boston Paranormal Society/Diane Okoye\|Diane Okoye]]'s collapse at Long Wharf on October 27 as a probable fourth. He notices the dates: **October 27, 29, 31, November 3.** *Two-day cadence with a gap at November 1.* Either there's a missed incident on the 1st, or the pattern is something else.

If you draw a circle through the four points, the center hovers around **historic downtown Boston** — Faneuil Hall, the Old North Church, the Public Garden, TD Garden, the Commons. Anyone working from a base around there could reach all four sites.

### The Writing

[[Player Characters/Bohdan Thorruk\|Bo]] looks at the photo of the frost-script on the monument and recognizes its phonetic shape — *similar to Druidic and Elvish, as if those languages branched off of this one in some distant timeline.* He's not Daniel Jackson. He can't read it. But he knows what it isn't.

> *"It might be… Fae?"*
> — **[[Player Characters/Bohdan Thorruk\|Bo]]**

> *"Oh great. So that would directly tie into everything being related here."*
> — **[[Player Characters/Chandler T. Harrow\|Chandler]]**

## Part Three: The Monument

The Soldier's Monument is directly across the street from [[Player Characters/Chandler T. Harrow\|Chandler]]'s apartment. The party walks there. Fifteen minutes.

The grass around the base is *slightly* less green than the rest of the Common. Whatever happened here on November 3 left a footprint — literally.

### Detect Magic + The Ecto Arcane Scanner

[[Player Characters/Chandler T. Harrow\|Chandler]] casts **Detect Magic as a ritual** while [[Player Characters/Leonard Holloway\|Leonard]] powers up his homemade **Ecto Arcane Scanner**. The moment [[Player Characters/Chandler T. Harrow\|Chandler]] holds the scanner himself, his innate magical channel boosts it like a battery, and the device starts shouting.

> *"Warning, a summoning occurred here. Warning, knowledge of the past, present, and future was attempted to be gleaned here. Warning, controlling of the mind, thoughts, and feelings happened here. Warning, the channeling of great power happened here."*
> — **Ecto Arcane Scanner**

Four schools — **Conjuration, Divination, Enchantment, and Evocation** — all detected in the same patch of grass.

> *"Yeah, I'm getting the exact same sense of what it's saying. Just with me trying to channel all my observation here. There's something freaky going on."*
> — **[[Player Characters/Chandler T. Harrow\|Chandler]]**

### What the Roll Reveals

[[Player Characters/Leonard Holloway\|Leonard]] rolls a **nat 20 Investigation** with [[Player Characters/Chandler T. Harrow\|Chandler]]'s help. Then [[Player Characters/Bohdan Thorruk\|Bo]] rolls a **22 Investigation** on the physical tracks. Between them:

- A pair of **footprints** in the dead grass directly in front of the monument, facing north. **Tall** — bigger than [[Player Characters/Jacques le Coq\|Jacques]]'s feet. **Narrow** — dress-shoe profile, not work boots.
- The **Conjuration** signature comes from where the footprints are. *Something was summoned here.*
- The **Divination + Enchantment** signatures form a **directed cone** radiating *away* from the footprints — the cone matches a shadow-outline of a humanoid figure mid-jog. *Whatever this was scanned [[NPCs/Harvard/Patricia Osei\|Patricia Osei]]'s mind and tried to influence her.*
- The **Evocation** signature is **between the footprints and the monument** — power channeled at the monument *to write the frost script on it.*

> *"There's some big-ass dude here, bigger than either one of you two guys. And he looks like he blasted some sort of magic out and hit our jogger lady. He wanted to know something, and he wanted to command her. Then it looks like he magically made the writing appear here with some more magic."*
> — **[[Player Characters/Chandler T. Harrow\|Chandler]]**

[[NPCs/Harvard/Patricia Osei\|Patricia Osei]] is, however, not catatonic the way [[NPCs/Boston Paranormal Society/Diane Okoye\|Diane]] was, and not described as foggy the way [[NPCs/Spectacle Island/Sapper Ripley\|Sapper Ripley]], [[NPCs/Spectacle Island/Mai Xiong\|Mai Xiong]], and [[NPCs/Spectacle Island/Monroe Tanner\|Monroe Tanner]] were on Spectacle Island. Either she resisted the Enchantment, or she didn't get the full dose. Or, as [[Player Characters/Bohdan Thorruk\|Bo]] notes:

> *"Maybe because she's a little bit more than human."*
> — **[[Player Characters/Bohdan Thorruk\|Bo]]**

## Part Four: Ranger Diggs

The ranger station next to the Frog Pond says CLOSED in the window. The party knocks anyway.

**[[NPCs/Boston Common/Ranger Diggs\|Ranger Diggs]]** answers. Mid-to-late 30s, no military bearing, no manual-labor calluses — smooth palms, with one currently coated in frog slime he wipes onto his pants without comment. He immediately makes [[NPCs/Harvard/Patricia Osei\|Patricia Osei]] sound like a personal disappointment.

> *"Yeah, she looked fine. I mean, she looked real fine — but that's just between you and me."*
> — **[[NPCs/Boston Common/Ranger Diggs\|Diggs]]**

[[Player Characters/Chandler T. Harrow\|Chandler]] flashes his Globe press credentials, says he's investigating "meteorological anomalies and the impending ice age." Diggs is not exactly a critical thinker.

> *"You know they're always watching, right? They got satellites out there. They can read the license plate off the back of your car, I'm telling you."*
> — **[[NPCs/Boston Common/Ranger Diggs\|Diggs]]**

> *"Only if your license plate pointed upward, buddy."*
> — **[[Player Characters/Chandler T. Harrow\|Chandler]]**

### What Diggs Saw

That morning, the air temperature was in the 40s. Frogs in the pond were sluggish. He heard shouting, walked toward the monument, and the temperature *dropped*. Could see his own breath. The fog was unnaturally thick **only around the monument**. And the frogs that morning were not just sluggish — many were *dead*.

He's been cleaning dead frogs out of the pond for a week.

He photographed the frost-writing on the monument with his Polaroid — full-resolution, not the photocopy O'Malley got. He hands it over.

> *"Squiggly bigglies."*
> — **[[NPCs/Boston Common/Ranger Diggs\|Diggs]]** *(professional technical term for the writing on the monument)*

The party leaves him to his frogs.

> *"It took every fiber in my being not to say, yes, the Earth is round, but at that angle, you wouldn't be able to read a license plate due to the distortion effect of the atmosphere."*
> — **[[Player Characters/Leonard Holloway\|Leonard]]** *(once they were out of earshot)*

## Part Seven: The Embassy

The party takes the T south to Bo's apartment. Sixty cents per fare. [[Player Characters/Lee Smith\|Lee]] hops the turnstile.

> *"It is the 1980s. It is not New York. It is really easy to hop the fucking turnstiles if you want to. And no, the police are busy."*
> — **GM**

### The Gingerbread House

[[Player Characters/Bohdan Thorruk\|Bo]] opens the door of his apartment to find:

- A full-scale, **magical-confectionery gingerbread house** built inside his Fae circle on the kitchen counter
- His radio relocated *inside* it, playing **ZZ Top**, muffled but audible
- The candy-gumdrop shingles vibrating to the music
- Approximately forty Fae partying inside it
- One particularly familiar fey at the door

The fey has had a productive week. She's also fashioned herself a glam-1980s outfit appropriate to the era. She greets [[Player Characters/Bohdan Thorruk\|Bo]] like a roommate who's been ghosted for ten days.

> *"Holy shit, where have you been?"*
> — **The fey**

> *"You've been gone all day. All day. I had to call all my friends. I had to have them build this house Amish style. You know how hard that shit is?"*
> — **The fey**

> *"There's more of you in there?"*
> — **[[Player Characters/Bohdan Thorruk\|Bo]]**

> *"Yeah, there's, like, forty of us in there."*
> — **The fey**

### "You May Call Me Very"

[[Player Characters/Bohdan Thorruk\|Bo]] tries again to get her name. She gives him a deliberately unhelpful answer.

> *"You may call me Very."*
> — **The fey**

> *"No, I'm going to call you Barry because I misheard you, and now you're Barry."*
> — **[[Player Characters/Bohdan Thorruk\|Bo]]**

> *"You know, I'm going to pretend you're saying 'Very' and I'm going to take it as a compliment. How about that?"*
> — **The fey**

### "An Agent of Espionage"

The fey casually reveals — when asked who her boss is — that her chain of command leads directly to **Queen Titania of the Summer Court.**

> *"My boss is Her Majesty, the Queen of Fire and Summer, Titania."*
> — **The fey**

> *"I mean, technically, I'm from the Spring Court — but that's besides the point. We fall under the jurisdiction of the Summer Court."*
> — **The fey**

She also clarifies, in passing, what she actually is.

> *"I would say less ambassador, more agent of espionage."*
> — **The fey**

> *"I like that better."*
> — **The fey** *(after a pause)*

She has been instructed to *observe* the party. Specifically. By name. All of them, individually — including [[Player Characters/Pierre-Jackson Laferrière\|Pierre]], who has never been to this apartment.

> *"My boss told me."*
> — **The fey** *(on how she knows everyone's names)*

> *"I am specifically deigned by Her Majesty to be here and observe specifically you and your cohorts for reasons that are unknown and will remain unknown to you."*
> — **The fey**

### The Photograph

[[Player Characters/Bohdan Thorruk\|Bo]] mimes a camera gesture at [[Player Characters/Leonard Holloway\|Leonard]]. [[Player Characters/Leonard Holloway\|Leonard]] aims his brand-new Polaroid One Step at the fey and clicks the red button.

> *"I did not give you permission to do that, young man."*
> — **The fey**

> *"We did that on our side of the boundary. I don't see the problem."*
> — **[[Player Characters/Bohdan Thorruk\|Bo]]**

The fey calculates the legal ramifications in real time — the boundary inside the boundary, the use of light-wave perception — and then issues a formal pricing notice.

> *"The next picture you take of me will cost you 500 United States dollars. I'm just letting you know. I am stating for legal precedent."*
> — **The fey**

The Polaroid ends up in [[Player Characters/Leonard Holloway\|Leonard]]'s journal.

### "What Does This Say?"

[[Player Characters/Bohdan Thorruk\|Bo]] shows her [[NPCs/Boston Common/Ranger Diggs\|Diggs]]'s photograph of the frost-script on Soldier's Monument and asks her to translate.

> *"Oh, oh, I'm not going to tell you that."*
> — **The fey**

He tries to negotiate. She wants:
- His **blood.** Twice asked, twice refused.
- A **television.** He can't afford one.
- A **plastic-cased radio with a remote.** Specifically plastic. Iron is dangerous to her.

She also confirms that the events the party witnessed on Spectacle Island count as *the party being in the wrong place at the wrong time*, not as a Fae attack on them — so they have no legal recourse to the courts.

> *"Subject has continued to investigate Fae-related endeavors. Has not been directly affected outside of their own penchant for being in the wrong place at the wrong time."*
> — **The fey** *(dictating into her notebook in real time)*

### The Iron

[[Player Characters/Bohdan Thorruk\|Bo]] has been preparing this since the Walgreens. He pulls out the bottle of **Flintstones Chewable Vitamins**, hands a few to [[Player Characters/Chandler T. Harrow\|Chandler]] as a prop, and asks [[Player Characters/Chandler T. Harrow\|Chandler]] to grind them up in his mortar. [[Player Characters/Chandler T. Harrow\|Chandler]] obliges — pretends he's interested in their nutritional value, dips a finger in the dust, calls them *invigorating*, and hands the paper back to [[Player Characters/Bohdan Thorruk\|Bo]] casually.

[[Player Characters/Bohdan Thorruk\|Bo]]'s plan: spike one of his open kombucha bottles with the iron-bearing vitamin powder. Then "accidentally" spill it toward the gingerbread house.

He calls for a distraction. [[Player Characters/Jacques le Coq\|Jacques]] obliges — *"Bo, can you pass me that on the back counter?"* — drawing the fey's eye. [[Player Characters/Bohdan Thorruk\|Bo]] rolls a **Sleight of Hand with advantage** and slides the vitamin powder into the kombucha. [[Player Characters/Lee Smith\|Lee]] notices via a **Cybernetically Bolstered Knack** psionic die. The fey rolls a **6 Perception.** She does not.

[[Player Characters/Bohdan Thorruk\|Bo]] picks up the spiked bottle, drinks from it, says *"oh, shit, this is pretty good — Flintstones and kombucha, who would have thought?"* and "accidentally" knocks it over. His already-permanent nosebleed kicks in at the same moment — adding a thin trickle of *actual blood iron* to the kombucha as it flows.

### The Banishment

The iron-laced kombucha pours across the counter toward the mushroom circle. The Fae circle dissolves on contact like an illusion. The whole embassy is forced back into the Feywild — the gingerbread house, the forty-fey party, the ZZ Top radio, and the fey herself.

She has just enough time to turn around.

> *"…nooo!"*
> — **The fey** *(disappearing)*

Her tiny plastic radio falls into the spreading puddle of kombucha and goes silent.

> *"Oh, no. Not again."*
> — **[[Player Characters/Bohdan Thorruk\|Bo]]** *(holding his bleeding nose, pretending not to notice)*

> [!note] The Polaroid Survives
> [[Player Characters/Leonard Holloway\|Leonard]] still has the picture of the fey. The fey herself is now back in the Feywild, presumably reporting up to Titania that her cover was blown by iron-laced kombucha. The cost of the next picture remains $500 in writing, in case she comes back for it.

*Session ends.*
